<p><p>A downdraft range hood is a kitchen appliance specifically designed to remove fumes, smoke, and odors that occur during cooking. Unlike conventional range hoods that are mounted above the cooking surface, downdraft range hoods pull the air downwards and exhaust it through ducts located either in the floor or behind the cooktop. This unique design is particularly beneficial in kitchen layouts where traditional range hood installation is challenging.</p><p>In terms of the current outlook, the downdraft range hoods market is experiencing steady growth due to various factors. Firstly, the increasing demand for modern and aesthetically pleasing kitchen designs has driven the adoption of downdraft range hoods, which are sleek and blend seamlessly into the kitchen countertop. Additionally, the growing focus on indoor air quality and health-conscious cooking practices has also contributed to the market's growth, as downdraft range hoods efficiently remove pollutants from the cooking environment.</p><p>Looking ahead, the future of the downdraft range hoods market appears promising. The trend of open kitchen layouts and the need for space-saving solutions in compact kitchens are expected to fuel the demand for downdraft range hoods. <p><p>Downdraft range hoods are available in two main market types: external blower and internal blower. In the external blower market, the range hood is designed to be connected to an external blower system located outside the kitchen. This setup allows for a more powerful and effective ventilation system. In the internal blower market, the range hood comes with a built-in blower that operates internally. This type is typically more compact and easier to install, making it suitable for kitchens without the option for an external blower system.</p></p>
