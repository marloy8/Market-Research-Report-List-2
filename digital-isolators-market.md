<p><p>Digital isolators are devices that provide electrical isolation between different circuitry while allowing digital signals to pass through. They use different types of coupling methods for achieving this isolation. Capacitive coupling isolates signals using capacitors, providing high-speed performance but limited noise immunity. Magnetic coupling isolators use transformers to transmit signals, offering better noise immunity but lower speed. Giant Magnetoresistive (GMR) isolators combine the advantages of both methods, utilizing magnetic fields for isolation while maintaining high-speed performance and noise immunity. These different types cater to varied requirements in the digital isolators market.</p></p>
