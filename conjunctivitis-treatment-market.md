<p><p>Conjunctivitis, also commonly known as pink eye, is an inflammation of the conjunctiva, the thin membrane that covers the white part of the eye. It can occur due to bacterial or viral infections, allergies, or exposure to irritants. Conjunctivitis is highly contagious and can be easily spread through direct contact with an infected person.</p><p>The treatment for conjunctivitis depends on its underlying cause. Bacterial conjunctivitis is often treated with antibiotic eye drops or ointments to eliminate the infection. Viral conjunctivitis, on the other hand, does not have a specific treatment and usually resolves on its own within a few days or weeks. Allergic conjunctivitis can be managed by avoiding the allergens and using antihistamine eye drops or oral medications to relieve the symptoms.</p><p>The conjunctivitis treatment market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years.
