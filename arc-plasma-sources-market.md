<p><p>Arc plasma sources are categorized into two types based on voltage: low voltage and high voltage. Low voltage arc plasma sources operate with lower electrical voltages, typically in the range of 100-200 volts. These sources are commonly used in various applications such as surface treatments, material deposition, and ion implantation. On the other hand, high voltage arc plasma sources operate with higher electrical voltages, usually exceeding 200 volts. High voltage sources are often utilized in industrial processes, such as plasma cutting, welding, and metal melting due to their ability to generate more intense plasma arcs.</p></p>
