<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The oil water separator market can be divided into three types: mechanical-based, chemical-based, and membrane-based. Mechanical-based separators operate on the principle of gravity separation, effectively removing oil from water based on their density difference. Chemical-based separators use coagulants or flocculants to form clumps of oil droplets, which can then be separated from water. Membrane-based separators use a porous physical barrier that allows water to pass through while trapping oil. Each type offers distinct advantages in terms of efficiency, cost-effectiveness, and ease of operation.</p></p>
