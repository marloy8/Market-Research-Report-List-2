<p><ul><li>Plasma-Enhanced Chemical Vapor Deposition (PECVD</li><li>Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD</li><li>Physical Vapor Deposition (PVD</li><li>Plasma-Enhanced Atomic Layer Deposition (PEALD)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Plasma generators are used in various industries for a range of applications. There are four main types of plasma generators in the market. The Plasma-Enhanced Chemical Vapor Deposition (PECVD) is a technique that uses plasma to deposit thin films on a substrate. Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) involves the formation of a chemical reaction using vaporized precursors. Physical Vapor Deposition (PVD) is a process where a solid material is evaporated and then deposited as a thin film. Plasma-Enhanced Atomic Layer Deposition (PEALD) combines the benefits of plasma and atomic layer deposition techniques to create thin films with precise control and uniformity.</p></p>
