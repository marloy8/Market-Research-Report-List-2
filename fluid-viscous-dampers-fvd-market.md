<p><p>Fluid viscous dampers (FVD) are devices used to absorb and dissipate the energy produced during earthquakes or vibrational events. The FVD market consists of two main types: linear type and non-linear type. Linear type FVDs provide a consistent damping force that is proportional to the velocity of the motion, whereas non-linear type FVDs provide a variable damping force that changes with the velocity. Both types find applications in structural engineering, civil construction, and seismic protection systems, offering enhanced safety and protection against structural damage.</p></p>
