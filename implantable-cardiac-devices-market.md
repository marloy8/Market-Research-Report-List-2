<p><p>Implantable Cardiac Devices (ICDs) are medical devices that are surgically implanted into the chest to help regulate and monitor the heart's electrical activity. These devices are primarily used to treat arrhythmias, which are irregular heart rhythms, and other cardiac conditions.</p><p>ICDs consist of two main components: a generator and one or more leads. The generator contains a battery and electronic circuits that monitor the heart's activity and deliver customized electric shocks or pacing if abnormal rhythms are detected. The leads are thin, insulated wires that deliver electrical signals between the generator and the heart.</p><p>The global implantable cardiac devices market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years. Factors driving the market growth include the increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, advancements in technology, rising geriatric population, and the growing demand for minimally invasive procedures. The market is also experiencing a shift towards the development of remote monitoring systems and smartphone-connected devices, allowing for improved patient care and convenience.</p><p>Furthermore, the market for implantable cardiac devices is driven by the increasing adoption of cardiac resynchronization therapy (CRT) devices and the rising demand for pacemakers and defibrillators.
