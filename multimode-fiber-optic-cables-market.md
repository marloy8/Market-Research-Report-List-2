<p><p>Multimode fiber optic cables are used in high-speed communication networks to transmit multiple signals simultaneously. These cables are categorized into different market types based on their construction. Step index multimode cables have a constant refractive index throughout their core, whereas gradient type cables have a varying refractive index from the core to the cladding. This variation in refractive index allows for better transmission of signals over longer distances with less signal loss. Both types are widely used in the telecommunications industry but differ in their transmission characteristics and performance.</p></p>
