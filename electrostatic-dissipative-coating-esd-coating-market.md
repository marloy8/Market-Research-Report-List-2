<p><p>Electrostatic Dissipative Coating (ESD Coating) is a specialized type of coating used to prevent the build-up of static electricity on surfaces. There are two main types of ESD coatings: water-based and solvent-based. Water-based ESD coatings are environmentally friendly and have low VOC content, making them suitable for sensitive applications. Solvent-based ESD coatings, on the other hand, offer excellent adhesion and durability but may have higher VOC emissions. Both types provide protective properties against static charges and are commonly used in industries that deal with sensitive electronic components, such as electronics manufacturing and telecommunications.</p></p>
