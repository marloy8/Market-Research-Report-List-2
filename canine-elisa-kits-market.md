<p><p>Canine ELISA Kits are used in veterinary diagnostics to detect the presence of specific antibodies or antigens in the blood of dogs. These kits come in different types, including unconjugated, alkaline phosphatase, biotin, and others. Unconjugated ELISA kits rely on the binding of an enzyme to the target molecule for detection. Alkaline phosphatase ELISA kits use the enzyme alkaline phosphatase to generate a color change when the target molecule is present. Biotin ELISA kits use biotinylated antibodies to capture the target molecule. Other types of ELISA kits may use different detection methods or conjugated enzymes for detection.</p></p>
