<p><p>The outdoor gas grill market consists of two primary types: propane gas and natural gas grills. Propane gas grills are powered by propane gas cylinders, providing convenience and portability as they can be easily moved and used in various outdoor settings. Natural gas grills, on the other hand, are connected to the main gas supply of a property, eliminating the need for propane cylinders and offering a continuous fuel source. The choice between these types depends on factors such as portability requirements, availability of natural gas connections, and personal preferences of the consumers.</p></p>
