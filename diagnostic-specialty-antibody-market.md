<p><p>The diagnostic specialty antibody market comprises three main types: monoclonal antibodies, polyclonal antibodies, and recombinant polyclonal antibodies. Monoclonal antibodies are created by cloning a single type of immune cell, ensuring high specificity. Polyclonal antibodies are derived from multiple immune cells and offer a broader range of recognition. Recombinant polyclonal antibodies are produced by introducing specific antibody genes into mammalian cells. These antibodies are advantageous due to their high specificity like monoclonal antibodies, but with the higher production yield obtained from polyclonal antibodies.</p></p>
