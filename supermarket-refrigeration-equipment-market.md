<p><p>The supermarket refrigeration equipment market can be categorized into two types: plug-in and remote systems. Plug-in systems refer to refrigeration units that are directly connected to an electrical outlet and are commonly used in smaller supermarket settings. On the other hand, remote systems involve a centralized refrigeration system that is located outside the store and connected to the display cases through piping. Remote systems are typically used in larger supermarkets or stores with multiple refrigeration units. Both types serve the purpose of keeping perishable items fresh and maintaining proper temperature control.</p></p>
