<p><p>Digital Mapping Cameras (DMC) are imaging systems used for capturing aerial or satellite images for the purpose of creating accurate digital maps. There are two main types of DMC market types: Linear Array Scanners (Pushbroom) and Area Arrays. Linear Array Scanners work by moving the sensor linearly to capture continuous strips of images, while Area Arrays capture images all at once using an array of sensors. Both types offer high-resolution imagery for mapping applications, but differ in the way they capture the images.</p></p>
