<p><p>The radiological detector market consists of two main types: fixed and handheld devices. Fixed detectors are installed in specific locations such as nuclear power plants and industrial facilities to continuously monitor radiation levels. They provide real-time data and are typically connected to a centralized monitoring system. On the other hand, handheld detectors are portable devices that can be carried by individuals for personal radiation detection. They are commonly used by emergency responders, security personnel, and in healthcare settings for radiation safety assessments and inspections. Both types play a crucial role in ensuring radiation safety in various industries and environments.</p></p>
