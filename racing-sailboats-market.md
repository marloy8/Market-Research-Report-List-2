<p><p>Racing sailboats can be broadly categorized into two types: monohull and multihull. Monohull sailboats have a single hull, making them more traditional and common in racing. They offer stability, speed, and performance in various wind conditions. On the other hand, multihull sailboats consist of multiple hulls, which provide enhanced speed and agility due to reduced drag. They are typically lighter, faster, and have a larger sail area, making them popular in high-performance racing events.</p></p>
