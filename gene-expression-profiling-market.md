<p><p>Gene expression profiling is a technique used to measure the activity of genes within a cell or tissue sample. This information helps researchers understand how genes are turned on and off, and how they are regulated. There are several different methods used in gene expression profiling, including RNA sequencing, Real-Time Quantitative PCR, and Digital PCR. RNA sequencing allows scientists to analyze the full transcriptome of cells, providing detailed information about gene expression levels. Real-Time Quantitative PCR is a more targeted approach, focusing on specific genes of interest. Digital PCR is a highly sensitive method that allows for absolute quantification of gene expression levels.</p></p>
