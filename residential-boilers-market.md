<p><p>Residential boilers are heating devices used in residential buildings to provide hot water and heating. Typically fueled by oil, natural gas, or propane, residential boilers are an integral part of central heating systems.</p><p>The residential boilers market is experiencing significant growth driven by several factors. The increasing demand for energy-efficient heating systems to reduce energy consumption and carbon emissions is one of the key drivers. Residential boilers offer high energy efficiency compared to traditional heating systems, making them an attractive option for homeowners.</p><p>Furthermore, the rising focus on smart homes and automation technologies is contributing to market growth. Modern residential boilers often come equipped with smart controls, allowing users to remotely control their heating systems. Manufacturers are also focusing on improving the durability and reliability of residential boilers to enhance their lifespan and reduce maintenance costs. Moreover, advancements in smart controls and automation technologies are expected to drive the demand for smart residential boilers in the coming years.</p></p>
