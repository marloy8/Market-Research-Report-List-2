<p><p>Organ transplantation immunosuppressive drugs are medications used to suppress the immune system of recipients of organ transplants. When a person receives an organ transplant, their immune system recognizes the new organ as foreign and tries to attack it. Immunosuppressive drugs are administered to prevent this immune response, helping to reduce the risk of organ rejection.</p><p>These drugs work by suppressing the activity of immune cells, including T cells, which are responsible for recognizing and attacking foreign substances. By dampening the immune response, these drugs allow the transplanted organ to function properly and improve the recipient's chances of long-term survival.</p><p>The global organ transplantation immunosuppressive drug market is projected to witness significant growth during the forecast period.
