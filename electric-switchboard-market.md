<p><p>The electric switchboard market is categorized into three types based on voltage levels: low-voltage, middle-voltage, and high-voltage markets. The low-voltage market refers to switchboards that operate at voltages up to 1000 volts. Middle-voltage switchboards operate at voltages ranging from 1000 to 38000 volts, while high-voltage switchboards operate at voltages above 38000 volts. These voltage levels determine the capacity and application of the switchboards. Low-voltage switchboards are commonly used in residential and commercial buildings, while middle and high-voltage switchboards are employed in industrial facilities and power distribution networks.</p></p>
