<p><p>A non-woven disc refers to a circular-shaped abrasive tool made of non-woven synthetic fibers, impregnated with abrasive materials such as aluminum oxide or silicon carbide. Firstly, the increasing demand for non-woven discs in the automotive industry is driving market growth. These discs are extensively used for surface preparation, paint removal, and rust removal in the automotive sector. Moreover, the rising trend of do-it-yourself (DIY) projects among consumers is also fueling the market's expansion. Non-woven discs are a popular choice among DIYers for sanding and polishing purposes.</p><p>Additionally, the growth of end-use industries such as metal fabrication, construction, and aerospace is boosting the demand for non-woven discs. These industries utilize non-woven discs for applications including weld blending, surface conditioning, and edge deburring.</p><p>The market's growth is further facilitated by technological advancements leading to improved product performance and durability. <p><ul><li>Quick Change Type</li><li>Arbor Hole Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Non-woven disc market types include Quick Change Type and Arbor Hole Type. The Quick Change Type discs are designed for easy and fast disc changes, allowing users to switch discs without the need for special tools. They are commonly used in industries where efficiency and speed are important. On the other hand, Arbor Hole Type discs are designed with a pre-punched center hole that fits onto a specific arbor or spindle. These discs are commonly used in applications where a secure and precise mounting is essential.</p></p>
