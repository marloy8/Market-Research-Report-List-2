<p><p>Dental filling materials are used to restore and fill cavities in teeth caused by tooth decay and dental caries. These materials help to prevent further tooth decay and restore the function and appearance of the affected tooth. Dental filling materials can be classified into direct and indirect restorative materials. Direct restorative materials are placed directly into the cavity, while indirect restorative materials are fabricated outside the mouth and then placed in the cavity.</p><p>The dental filling materials market has been experiencing significant growth due to the rising prevalence of dental caries, an aging population, and increasing dental healthcare expenditure. <p><p>The dental filling materials market encompasses various types such as silver amalgam, composite resin, glass ionomer cements, and others. Silver amalgam fillings have been used traditionally due to their durability but are less popular due to their metallic appearance. Composite resin fillings are aesthetically appealing and chemically bonded to the tooth, making them a popular choice. Glass ionomer cements release fluoride and are often used for children. Other materials include ceramics and gold. Each material possesses unique characteristics, allowing dentists to choose the most suitable option based on patient needs.</p></p>
