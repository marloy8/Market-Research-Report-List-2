<p><p>The Clinical Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) market consists of various types, including Targeted Sequencing, Exome Sequencing, and Others. Targeted Sequencing specifically focuses on selected regions of DNA, enabling researchers to identify specific genetic variations. Exome Sequencing examines only the protein-coding regions of DNA, providing insights into genetic mutations that may cause diseases. The Other category encompasses various sequencing approaches, such as Whole Genome Sequencing, which looks at the entire genome, and RNA Sequencing, which analyzes gene expression levels. These different types of NGS offer various applications and benefits in clinical research and diagnostics.</p></p>
