<p><p>Hospital Real-time Location Systems (RTLS) are used to accurately track the location of patients, staff, and medical equipment within a healthcare facility. There are several types of technologies used in the RTLS market:</p><p>1. RFID (Radio Frequency Identification): This system uses small tags attached to people or objects that emit radio signals, allowing for precise tracking and monitoring.</p><p>2. Wi-Fi: Utilizing existing Wi-Fi infrastructure, this system can locate devices and individuals by detecting their Wi-Fi signals.</p><p>3. Ultrasound: Using sound waves, ultrasound-based RTLS enables accurate indoor positioning and tracking.</p><p>4. Infrared: Infrared sensors detect heat emitted by people or objects, enabling precise location identification.</p><p>5. Other: This category includes various emerging technologies such as Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) and Zigbee, which offer alternative tracking solutions for RTLS in hospitals.</p></p>
