<p><p>Copper mining is conducted through two major market types: underground mining and open pit mining. Underground mining involves excavating tunnels and shafts to reach copper deposits deep below the surface. This method is typically used when copper resources are located at substantial depths. On the other hand, open pit mining involves the removal of topsoil and rock layers to expose copper deposits near the surface. It is commonly employed when copper ores are abundant and easily accessible, requiring large-scale machinery and equipment for extraction.</p></p>
