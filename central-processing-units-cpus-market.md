BGA CPUs utilize an array of solder balls to connect them to the motherboard, providing a more secure and reliable connection. On the other hand, CSP CPUs are smaller and use advanced packaging techniques, allowing for higher integration and reduced size. Both BGA and CSP markets cater to different needs and preferences in terms of performance, size, and connectivity in the CPU market.</p></p>
