<p><p>Peer-to-peer (P2P) lending, also known as crowdfunding or marketplace lending, is an alternative form of lending and investment that connects borrowers directly with individual lenders through online platforms. It eliminates the need for traditional financial institutions, such as banks, by facilitating direct lending relationships between individuals. P2P lending allows borrowers to easily access loans at competitive interest rates, while lenders can earn higher returns compared to traditional investment options.</p><p>The global P2P lending market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue its upward trajectory. The market growth can be attributed to several factors, including increased internet penetration, rising awareness and trust in online lending platforms, streamlined loan application processes, and the ability to offer lower interest rates compared to traditional banks.</p><p>Furthermore, technological advancements, such as the integration of artificial intelligence and machine learning in credit scoring and risk assessment, have improved the efficiency and accuracy of P2P lending platforms. This has increased their popularity among both borrowers and lenders, further driving market growth.</p><p>In terms of market trends, the P2P lending industry has experienced a shift towards institutional participation. Traditional financial institutions and institutional investors are increasingly entering the P2P lending space, either by forming partnerships with existing platforms or by establishing their own lending platforms. This trend has brought additional credibility and stability to the market, attracting more borrowers and lenders.</p><p>Moreover, the P2P lending market has seen the emergence of niche lending platforms catering to specific needs, such as small business loans, student loans, and real estate financing. These specialized platforms provide borrowers and lenders with tailored solutions, resulting in increased market segmentation.</p><p>Overall, the P2P lending market is expected to continue its growth at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 11.5% during the forecast period.
