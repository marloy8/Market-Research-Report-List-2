<p><p>Contact smart cards are divided into two main types: active cards and passive cards. </p><p>Active cards require an external power source, such as a battery, to function. They have the ability to perform complex tasks and calculations, making them suitable for applications that demand high security and processing power. </p><p>On the other hand, passive cards do not have an internal power source and rely on an external reader to provide the necessary power. They have limited processing capabilities and are typically used for simpler tasks like access control or payment transactions.</p></p>
