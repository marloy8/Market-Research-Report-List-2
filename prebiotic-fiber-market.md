<p><p>Prebiotic fiber refers to certain types of dietary fibers that can stimulate the growth and activity of beneficial bacteria in the gut, promoting digestive health. Inulin, Mannan-Oligosaccharide (MOS), Fructo-Oligosaccharide (FOS), and Galacto-Oligosaccharide (GOS) are examples of prebiotic fibers. Inulin is primarily sourced from chicory roots and is commonly used as a food ingredient due to its soluble fiber properties. MOS is derived from the yeast cell wall and helps support healthy digestion and immune function. FOS, found in many fruits and vegetables, promotes the growth of beneficial bacteria. GOS, mainly sourced from fermented milk, has similar prebiotic effects and supports gut health.</p></p>
