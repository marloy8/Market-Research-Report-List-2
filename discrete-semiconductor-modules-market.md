<p><p>The Discrete Semiconductor Modules market consists of different types, including Standard (Non-Integrated) IGBT Modules, Intelligent Power Modules, Thyristor/Diode Modules, Power Integrated Modules, and MOSFET Modules.</p><p>Standard (Non-Integrated) IGBT Modules are standalone devices that combine an insulated-gate bipolar transistor (IGBT) with a freewheeling diode. They provide high voltage and current capability for power-oriented applications.</p><p>Intelligent Power Modules are advanced modules that integrate control, drive, and protection circuits along with power devices. These modules offer enhanced functionality, reliability, and efficiency compared to traditional modules.</p><p>Thyristor/Diode Modules are modules that combine thyristors and diodes to provide controlled rectification or switching in power electronic systems.</p><p>Power Integrated Modules are modules that integrate multiple power devices, such as IGBTs, diodes, and MOSFETs, along with control and protection circuits. These modules offer improved power density and performance in compact designs.</p><p>MOSFET Modules consist of metal-oxide-semiconductor field-effect transistors (MOSFETs) and provide efficient switching capabilities for power electronic applications, delivering high power density and low conduction losses.</p></p>
